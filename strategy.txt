HUNTER EXAM OPTIMIZATION STRATEGY

The system models the Hunter Exam as a sequence of probabilistic elimination phases.

1. Modeling:
Each applicant has stats and energy. Each phase applies weighted scoring and removes a percentage of participants.

2. Optimization:
A Monte Carlo simulation is used (100 runs). Survival probability is estimated by repeated trials.

3. Energy Strategy:
The system alternates between exertion and conservation:
- Conserve early to preserve energy
- Exert in critical phases

4. Alliances:
Random small alliances are formed to simulate uncertainty. Alliances provide a multiplier bonus.

5. Wildcards:
Modeled using random stat variation (±30%)

6. Ringers:
Implicitly detected via strong performance compared to baseline stats

7. Tradeoffs:
- Aggressive play increases short-term survival but risks exhaustion
- Conservative play improves endurance but risks elimination early

The chosen strategy balances both.
